Vegetable Soup

About Vegetable Soup

This is Nigerian cuisine also known as Edikang Ikong. This is a nutritious, tasty, leafy green vegetable soup from Southern Nigeria. It is made with fluted pumpkin leaves and waterleaf. The original version of this soup is made with water leaves and fluted pumpkin leaves (Ugu).

This should not be mistaken for Afang soup.I can't really tell the difference by look just by taste. I'm gonna share how i make mine.

Ingredients

  • Ugu leaves
  • Water leaves
  • Beef
  • Pepper and salt
  • Stockfish
  • Kpomooptional
  • Palm oil
  • onions

Steps

Prep your ingredients, wash leaves properly, chop the veggies. Preheat the oven at 350F for 10 to 15mins

  1. Get a pot, add the washed beef, onion, salt, cube, stockfish and kpomo. Put on the stove, medium heat and boil.
  2. Add more water when liquid is almost dry and let it boil
  3. When the water is very litte, turn off the stove, remove proteins in the pot and set aside.
  4. Put the pot back on the stove add palm oil(this serves as the liquid ) let it heat for a while, then add pepper and boil
  5. Add the vegetables and stir till even and close the pot boil. avoid boiling for a long time so the leave can stiil be fresh
  6. Add the proteins, then add salt to taste if needed. Stir the content and turn off the heat

Our Edikan Ikong soup is prepared and read to be eaten, now this can be served with any swallow of your choice, mine is pounded yam, also eaten with white rice or yam.
